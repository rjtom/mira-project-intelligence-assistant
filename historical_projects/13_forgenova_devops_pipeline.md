# ForgeNova DevOps Pipeline Transformation
**Project Type:** Internal IT
**Company:** ForgeNova Automotive
**Timeline:** Q1 2024 – Q4 2025 (24 months)
**Status:** In Progress (Phase 2)
## Overview
Modernization of software development and deployment processes through advanced DevOps practices, enabling faster, more reliable software delivery across ForgeNova's engineering teams.
## Objectives & Success Criteria
- Increase development speed while maintaining quality standards
- Improve team collaboration and developer satisfaction
- Deliver more reliable software to the business
- Reduce deployment failures and mean time to recovery
## Detailed Timeline & Milestones
- **Q1 2024**: DevOps tooling assessment and pipeline design
- **Q2 2024**: CI/CD pipeline implementation and initial rollout
- **Q3 2024**: Process redesign and team training
- **Q4 2024**: Full tooling adoption across all development teams
- **Q1 2025**: Continuous improvement and metrics tracking
- **Q4 2025**: Full adoption and optimization complete
## Resource Requirements
- Approximately 120 team members across DevOps engineering, software development, and quality assurance
- Dedicated DevOps platform engineering team of 15 engineers
- Significant investment in CI/CD tooling, cloud infrastructure, and automation platforms
- Collaboration with external DevOps consultancy partners
- Training programs for all development teams on new processes and tools
## Governance & HIL Checkpoints
- Regular team feedback and workload reviews
- Monthly leadership reviews on adoption metrics and team well-being
- Key decision points requiring human judgment on process changes affecting developer workflows
- Regular reflection sessions on team impact and creativity
## Critical Thinking Analysis
- Assumptions Challenged: That automation would remove all bottlenecks without affecting people
- Perception & Reality Check: How do these changes affect developer well-being and creativity?
- Love as Derived Work: Does this create better conditions for people to do meaningful work?
## Lessons Learned
A reflection of genuine insight: The best processes support human creativity rather than constrain it. Automation should serve people — not the other way around. Developer well-being and psychological safety are as important as pipeline speed metrics.
